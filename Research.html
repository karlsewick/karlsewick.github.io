Explanation of pursuits

My research from before college predominantly revolved around phenomena that aren't entirely understood but are not significant enough to have professors researched. I studied why clothes get darker when wet with water, development of a contactless motor using magnetic fields, and creating a circle of light from a laser and a wire. This research really exposed me to understanding the theory behind these topics (single-slit diffraction, total internal reflection, faraday's law, etc.) but also helped me understand how to design experimental setups, accomodate all variability, and much more.

The research I've done in college has been much different. In engineering at the University of Pittsburgh, we are required to complete a lot of papers regarding different topics. Some of these papers covered topics like efficient nuclear fusion/fission, organic enhanced photovoltaics, as well as topics in the fields of advanced hardware and complex signal transfer.
